About Sister Vivian

A journey of faith, dedication, and service to humanity

Passionate About Healthcare & Service

Hello! I'm Vivian Antwi, affectionately known as Sister Vivian. I'm currently pursuing my Bachelor of Science in Nursing at KAAF University College in Kasoa, where I'm committed to developing the skills and knowledge needed to provide exceptional, compassionate healthcare.

My journey has been guided by a deep commitment to faith and service. As a devoted member of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints, I dedicated 18 months to missionary service after completing secondary school—an experience that profoundly shaped my character and reinforced my dedication to serving others.

During my time at Anlo Awomefia Senior High School, I served as the Entertainment Prefect, discovering my passion for bringing people together and creating memorable experiences. This leadership role taught me the importance of creativity, organization, and community building.

My Calling

Mission Service Experience

A transformative journey of faith, service, and personal growth

After completing my secondary education at Anlo Awomefia Senior High School, I embarked on one of the most transformative experiences of my life—serving as a full-time missionary for The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints. This 18-month period of dedicated service was more than just a commitment; it was a profound journey that shaped who I am today.

"The best way to find yourself is to lose yourself in the service of others."

My mission taught me the profound truth of this principle. Every day was an opportunity to serve, teach, uplift others, and discover my own strength, purpose, and capacity for compassion.

This experience prepared me not only for a life of continued service but also for my chosen career in nursing, where empathy, dedication, and the desire to help others are essential qualities.
